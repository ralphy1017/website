Rafael Ortiz III

PhD Researcher · Arizona State University
I study the co-evolution of supermassive black holes and galaxies using deep X-ray surveys, JWST imaging, and multiwavelength SED fitting. My work focuses on characterizing X-ray selected AGN, their host-galaxy properties, and AGN candidates during the epoch of reionization.

Research Focus

Multiwavelength AGN science from cosmic dawn to present day

⚡ X-ray Selected AGN

Deep Chandra/XMM-Newton surveys to characterize AGN populations, obscuration (NH distributions), and ultrafast outflows across multiple deep fields.

🌌 Multiwavelength SED Fitting

Decomposing AGN and stellar contributions across UV–IR using CIGALE to constrain SFRs, stellar masses, and AGN luminosities for large host-galaxy samples.

🔬 JWST PEARLS Survey

Deep NIRCam multi-band imaging across the NEP-TDF, MACS-0416, and El Gordo to study faint galaxies, AGN candidates, and radio-counterpart identification at z > 4.

Research

Active projects and ongoing investigations

⚡ X-ray AGN Catalog Analysis

Building multiwavelength catalogs of X-ray selected AGN using Chandra and XMM-Newton archival data. Characterizing Compton-thin vs. Compton-thick populations and deriving NH column density distributions.

ChandraXMM-NewtonNHCompton-thick

🌌 SED Modeling of AGN Hosts

Applying CIGALE to UV–IR photometry of X-ray AGN samples to disentangle stellar and AGN emission, deriving stellar masses and star formation rates for statistical population studies.

CIGALEStellar MassSFRAGN Fraction

🔭 JWST PEARLS GTO Program

Contributing to data reduction, catalog construction, and science analysis across PEARLS fields. Identifying AGN counterparts to microjansky radio sources in the NEP Time-Domain Field.

JWSTPEARLSNEP-TDFRadio Sources

🌠 Reionization-Era AGN

Systematic search for AGN at z = 6.5–12 via photometric SED fitting in PEARLS and CEERS fields. Constraining AGN number densities and their role in cosmic reionization.

z>6ReionizationCEERSPEARLS

🔗 Galaxy Mergers & AGN Triggering

Investigating merger-triggered AGN activity and star formation at z = 4.5–8.5 using JWST close-pair statistics across CEERS, JADES, PEARLS, and five other survey fields.

Galaxy MergersAGN TriggeringClose PairsJADES

📡 Catalog Cross-Matching

Cross-matching X-ray, optical, near-IR, and radio catalogs using likelihood-ratio and Bayesian positional methods. Building robust multiwavelength source tables for downstream statistical analysis.

Catalog MatchingBayesianAstropyPython
