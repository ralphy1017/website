JWST PEARLS

jwstpearls ↗

PEARLS is a JWST Guaranteed Time Observation (GTO) program (PI: R. Windhorst, ASU) imaging multiple deep fields — including the North Ecliptic Pole Time-Domain Field, MACS-0416, and El Gordo — in up to eight NIRCam bands. My contributions include identifying and characterizing Seyfert galaxies in the JWST NEP TDF with central point-like emission from the AGN, cross-matching X-ray and radio sources with UV-Optical-IR counterparts from space- and ground-based data, and SED fitting with CIGALE to constrain AGN and host-galaxy properties across cosmic time. I am also a "Builder" due to my contributions to the data pipeline and processing.

CIGALE Chandra XMM-Newton NIRCam NEP-TDF High-z AGN
🌌

Project DARK-SKY / SKYSURF-IR

Building on the HST SKYSURF legacy project, DARK-SKY (JWST Cycle 3 AR, PI: R. Windhorst) analyzes >60,000 NIRCam and NIRISS images across 100 independent JWST fields to measure the absolute 0.9–5.0 μm sky surface brightness and set competitive limits on diffuse Extragalactic Background Light (EBL). My role involves spearheading the processing of the entire JWST NIRCam/NIRISS database up to October 2024, which includes calibrating individual exposure files and producing single-visit mosaics. In addition, I produced panchromatic sky-surface brightness measurements, Zodiacal Light and Diffuse Galactic Light modeling, and data quality assessment.

JWST NIRCam NIRISS Zodiacal Light DGL EBL ProFound Sky-SB Calibration
🪐

JWST VENUS — Very Early and Nascent Universe Survey

jwst-venus ↗

VENUS (PIs: S. Fujimoto, U. Toronto & D. Coe, STScI) is a JWST survey with 150+ international members targeting galaxy formation at z ≳ 14 through gravitational lensing. The program probes ultra-faint galaxies down to MUV ≃ −12, conducts the first systematic census of low-mass black holes (down to ~105 M⊙) in the early universe, and opens a time-domain window on lensed stars and supernovae at z ≳ 5. Observations are ongoing with a first data release anticipated in late 2026.
