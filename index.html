Studying Active Galactic Nuclei and extragalactic science using multiwavelength observations with JWST, XMM-Newton, and Chandra. Focused on AGN-galaxy coevolution, X-ray selected AGN, and SED modeling at Arizona State University.

Research Focus

My work spans multiwavelength AGN science, utilizing cutting-edge space telescopes to understand black hole growth and galaxy evolution.

Project PEARLS - AGN Science

Leading multiwavelength AGN research using JWST/NIRCam in the North Ecliptic Pole Time Domain Field. Discovery and characterization of point-source features in galaxy cores at z < 3, X-ray selected AGN analysis with XMM-Newton, and SED fitting with X-CIGALE to constrain photometric redshifts, stellar masses, and AGN properties. Investigating AGN-galaxy coevolution, obscured accretion, and the role of feedback in galaxy evolution.

JWST NIRCam X-ray AGN SED Fitting XMM-Newton Coevolution Obscuration

Project SKYSURF-IR - Background Analysis

Leading data pipeline development and sky surface brightness measurements across 60,000+ JWST near-infrared images. Measuring panchromatic sky-SB and modeling astrophysical foregrounds/backgrounds to constrain diffuse light sources. Coordinating with NASA's JWST Backgrounds Team to characterize near-IR backgrounds and optimize data reduction techniques for large-scale JWST archival analysis.
